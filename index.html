Causes of Deaths in Australia

Visualisation by: Helena Tam Hoy Loon

Australia, as a developed country, is well-known for its health system such that it is one of the country over the world that has a high life expectancy.

There are many factors that can cause mortality, and they can be divided into two main categories:

Mortality due to Diseases or Sickness and Mortality due to External Causes or Accidents


Relative Ranking of Death Rates in Australia by States (2011-2020)

It is shown that the standardised death rate of different states in Australia is considered constant over years.

Tasmania has an overall highest death rate over years - 8.56 deaths per 1,000 persons.

Northern Territory has an overall lowest death rate over years - 4.51 deaths per 1,000 persons.

The average death rate per 1,000 population of resident of Australia ranges around 4 ~ 9.


Deaths Caused by Diseases

There are many chronic diseases that can lead to deaths.

In order to reduce death rate, an analysis on the major diseases that lead to death in Australia has been done by Australia Government so that they can put in effort in the correct path.

The graphs below this section provides an analysis on the major diseases that cause mortality and the analysis of the top 3 diseases in Australia.


Top 10 Underlying Causes of Deaths by Disease in Australia (2011-2020)

It is shown that the top 10 diseases in Australia that causes death remains the same from 2012 to 2020.

The top 3 diseases that contributes to most of the deaths of Australia citizens are:

1. Ischaemic Heart Disease,

2. Malignant Neoplasms of Digestive Organs

3. Cerebrovascular Diseases.

Deaths Caused by External Factors

However, other than diseases, deaths might also occur due to unexpected injuries, accidents, self-abuse, mental issues and many other reasons.

These reasons of deaths, as a whole, is known as the "External Causes of Deaths".

The graphs below this section provides an analysis on the major external causes of mortality and the analysis of the cause that leads to highest number of deaths.


Top 8 External Causes of Deaths in Australia (2011-2020)

In overall, the external cause that leads to highest number of deaths over years is due to Intentional Self Harm (X60-X84).

The cause that leads to second highest number of deaths over years is due to Falls (W00-W19).

Number of deaths due to intensional self harm, falls and noxious substances poisoning has been increasing over years.

Number of deaths due to car accident, assault, motorcycle accident, undetermined intent and breathing threats are considered constant over years.


Number of Deaths due to Intensional Harm (X60-X84) by States and Gender in Australia (2011-2020)

Northern Territory has an overall higher death rate over years.

New South Wales and Victoria has an overall lower death rate over years.

The death rate in years 2015, 2017 and 2019 are overall higher.

Male in Northern Territory has an overall higher death rate over years.

Death rates for male across all states have an average of > 18 deaths per 100,000 population residents over years.

By observing the scale domain, male's death rate are higher than female's death rate for all states over years.

Death rates for female across all states are overall constant with an average of < 10 deaths per 100,000 population residents over years,

Except for Northern Territory that has exceptionally higher death rate on years 2014, 2015, 2019 and 2020

By observing the scale domain, female's death rate are lower than male's death rate for all states over years.


Number of Deaths due to Intensional Harm (X60-X84) by Age Group in Australia (2011-2020)

Age group of top 3 highest number of deaths due to intensional harm or suicide are:

1. 45 - 54

2. 35 - 34

3. 25 - 24

According to Australian Bureau of Statistic (ABS), there are many leading causes of suicide including:

mood disorder, anxiety and stress, family issue, employment and unemployment, economy problems, acute substances use of intoxication and spousal relationship problems.

These problems are common among these three age group, therefore, it is unsurpising that these age group have hight number of suicide cases.
